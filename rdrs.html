How to Clean (or replace) an (NEC) Projector

Estimated Time: 5-10 mins.

Note: After physically cleaning or replacing the filter, you must reset the filter hours on the projector.

Cleaning the filter
  • Remove the filter covers on the side of the projector.
  • Remove the filter from the cover.
  • Beat the dust from the filters into a trash can.
  • Place the filters back onto the covers.
  • Place the covers with the filters back onto the projector.
  • See below to refer how to reset the filter hours on the projector.
Replacing the filter
  • Remove the filter covers on the side of the projector.
  • Remove the filter from the cover.
  • Discard of the old filters into the trash.
  • Cut out new filters that will fit the covers.
  • Place the filters back onto the covers.
  • Place the covers with the filters back onto the projector.
  • See below to refer how to reset the filter hours on the projector.
Resetting the filter hours on the projector
  • Hit Menu on the NEC Projector remote.
  • Use the remote to navigate to Reset and hit Enter.
  • Use the remote to navigate to Filter Hours and hit Enter.
  • Use the remote to confirm the action.
  • Hit Exit on the remote until the menu is gone.

Setting-Up a Web-ex Call

Estimated Time: 5-10 mins.

Note: To use Web-ex in HDB 1.208, you must have X-Panel installed.

Note: To share content in HLB 1.111, you must press Share Local Content.

Getting the Call Started
  • Press the Video Conference call on the touchpanel or X-Panel.
  • Obtain the number to call in to the meeting.
  • Type in the number to the proper field and add @dellmed... (Typing @ should autopopulate.)
  • Audio should naturally be synced up with the mics.
To Set-up Sharing a Source
  • Press the Share button on the touchpanel or X-Panel.
  • Select which source should be shared.
Adjusting the Layout and Camera Angle
  • Use the remote and select the layout or camera angle button in the top right corner.

How to Swap Out a Projector

Estimated Time: 45 mins - 1 hour

Note: The projector you are replacing should be the same model as the replacement.

Removing the Old Projector
  • Turn off the system and the projector.
  • Use the remote and take a picture of the network settings of the projector.
  • Remove network and power cables from the projector.
  • Unscrew the mount and carefully lower the projector from the ceiling.
  • Remove the mount from the old projector.
Mounting the new Projector
  • Place the mount onto the new projector.
  • Screw the mount back carefully into the ceiling.
  • Insert the power and network cables into the new projector.
  • Aim the projector with an appropriate test pattern.
  • Configure the network settings so the old projector has the same info as the old one.

How to Update Projector Software

Estimated Time: 25-30 mins.

Note: Check the Firmware by Menu > Status > Projector Set-up.

Preparing the Update
  • Put the projector into stand-by mode.
  • Connect the projector to a computer via a RS232-USB cable.
  • Download (or open) the MainSubNet Update Tool from the projector's service home page.
  • Download the appropriate image for the projector.
Updating the Projector
  • Start-up the software with a computer, select Model, and then click [OK].
  • Select the Serial Radio Button.
  • Enter the data for the ID, Port, Baudrate, and Parity.
  • Check the connection with [Connection Check].
  • Verify the results of the connection check. If failed, verify the data.
  • Select the Microprocessor to update, and click [Load].
  • Load in the proper image for the projector.
  • Press update to begin the process. (This step takes around 10-15 mins.)
  • Press [OK] on the completion screen.
  • Press [Exit] once the update has finished.
  • Reboot the projector and confirm the Microprocessor updated.

Update Settings on a Computer

Estimated Time: 10-15 mins.

Note: To make resolution changes to OSX, use the packages provided on the USB stick.

Log in to Stache
  • Log in to Stache - if needed.
Update Settings on Mac Side
  • Log-in with atsadmin.
  • Open up Deep Freeze and log-in with atsadmin.
  • Reboot the computer thawed - if needed.
  • Re-open Deep Freeze - if needed.
  • Update the desired settings.
  • Apply the settings in Deep Freeze.
  • Select to boot frozen.
  • Reboot the computer.

  • Log-in with .\atsadmin.
  • Update the desired settings.
